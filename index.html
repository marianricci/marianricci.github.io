Professional Ethics

Your well-being is my number one priority and together we will work to achieve your goals. My therapeutic style is compassionate, relaxed, respectful, and non-judgmental, with strict confidentiality.
My psychotherapeutic approach consists of working together to provide a genuine, empathetic, and mutual understanding of the person's difficulties, focused on developing their personal growth, enriching, and enhancing the meaning of relationships with themselves and others.

Adolescents

Adolescence is often a difficult stage, both for the adolescent and those around them, due to the changes they face during this period. The goal of therapy is to help the adolescent restore their well-being in a short time. Brief Therapy addresses imbalances dynamically, flexibly, and effectively.

Adults

The goal of treatment is to increase understanding of the sources of our internal conflicts and emotional problems. This approach allows the pace of the sessions to be set by the patient while the therapist offers opportunities for the patient to gain a better understanding and make better decisions.

Children

Play is the child's language, which, when used therapeutically, allows them to master situations that cause them suffering. It is also a means of communication and a vehicle to express their desires, fears, and concerns. Children require the support and commitment of their parents for therapy to achieve the best results.

What do psychology therapies consist of?

The therapies I offer focus on dealing with feelings and thoughts that distress the person and understanding their ways of feeling, thinking, and acting. Sometimes these problems are due to past traumas, or to the person's relationship with themselves, which may be adversely affecting current relationships, self-image, and self-worth. Through this process, we will explore in depth those beliefs, important life events, themes, and repeating patterns that have emerged during your personal history.

Psychotherapy will help you gain a deeper understanding and a clearer internal perspective of your personal problems, so you can identify more resilient ways of dealing with difficulties, and even change certain aspects of yourself or your life.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I access Medicare benefits?

To access Medicare Better Access Mental Health rebates, you must request a mental health plan from your general practitioner, and a referral letter, in the name of the mental health provider you choose. This way, the doctor authorizes the rebate for a certain number of sessions (initially 6, with the possibility of extending up to 10 per year). It is worth mentioning that the rebate will only cover a percentage of the total session amount.

How long do the sessions last?

Each session lasts approximately 50 minutes.

How often should I go to therapy?

Usually once a week; however, this is negotiable with each patient as needed.

How many sessions will be necessary?

The duration of therapy is different for each person and largely depends on the goals to be achieved. Short therapies (6 - 10 sessions) are mostly used to address specific problems or to learn some strategies. Longer periods are required to work on deeper psychological problems, long-term issues, and behavior patterns established over the years. The duration of your therapy will be evaluated and discussed with you during the first session.
